My process

01
Understand the question first

Before touching any data — what decision does this analysis need to enable? A sharp question produces a useful answer. A vague brief produces a beautiful dashboard nobody acts on.

02
Audit & clean ruthlessly

Real-world data is always messier than expected. I document every cleaning decision so results are reproducible, auditable, and defensible when someone questions a number.

03
Explore without a hypothesis

Genuine exploratory analysis — no confirmation bias. I let the data surface surprises before moving to structure. The best insights usually weren't in the original brief.

04
Build for the decision-maker

Dashboard, report, or notebook — designed for the person who has to act on it, not the person who ran the analysis. If they need a PhD to read it, it hasn't been finished.

05
Deliver with narrative

Every deliverable comes with a plain-language summary: what the data shows, why it matters, and what to do next. Numbers without story get filed and forgotten.
